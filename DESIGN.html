§ 1The problem the stack solves

Two repos exist today; neither is the right answer on its own. bluemarble-ai-framework v0 is a clean library design with no production miles. bm-ai-platform develop has all the production miles but is gated by Comviva-internal dependencies and a "platform" framing it doesn't deserve.

Figure 1 · Why merge — neither source is the right answer on its own

§ 2Two-band architecture

The single load-bearing decision of v3. The SDK is the product; the reference distribution is supported. Same repo, distinct altitude, separate release cadence. Band 2 depends on Band 1, never the reverse.

Figure 2 · The two bands — library is the heart, deployable is supported

Why the bands?

The Framework Advocate said "library, never platform." The Platform Advocate said "go look at examples/ kills procurement." Both were right. Two visual altitudes is the compromise both can defend.

One-way dependency

Band 2 imports Band 1. Band 1 never imports Band 2. A CI guardrail enforces this — same shape as care-intelligence's isolation gate.

Release cadence

SDK majors gate distribution majors, not vice versa. The library can ship a minor that the distribution hasn't picked up yet; never the reverse.

Step 3 closes the leak

The multi-tenant data-leak the Platform Advocate warned about. Header alone never determines pack; the token's tenant claim must match. Mismatch returns 401, never 200.

Step 5 enables observability

Once OTel baggage is set, every downstream span carries pack/use-case/tenant attributes. Langfuse can slice by any of them; AI-Trail captures the same correlation_id.

Step 7 is library-mode

No central model proxy. The use-case process calls LiteLLM directly. No SPOF, no gateway team, no latency hop.

§ 6The 15-minute prompt-hotfix lane

The Platform Advocate's strongest argument was Friday-night ops pain. The Framework Advocate's strongest counter was the regulator audit hole. The resolution: a documented hotfix lane that gives both sides what they need — fast prod, full review trail.

Win pattern

Framework won when…

The question was about internal discipline — what the SDK looks like, where telemetry lives, how prompts are reviewed. The library-first doctrine held.

Platform won when…

The question was about customer-site reality — what gets deployed, how procurement signs off, how operators see what's happening at 2am.

Compromise when…

Both positions had load-bearing weight. The two-band architecture and the two-blessed-orchestrator pattern both fall here.

§ 9Telemetry vs audit — two streams, one capture

A subtle but load-bearing distinction. OpenLLMetry is for engineers debugging behavior. AI-Trail is for regulators and ops asking "what did the agent do for ticket #INC-4421." Same capture, two sinks, different schemas.

Figure 8 · One capture, two sinks — telemetry and audit don't share a code path in the use case
